About Us Who we are and what we do

Company Overview

Habitat for Humanity International is an ecumenical Christian ministry that welcomes to its work all people dedicated to the cause of eliminating poverty housing. Since its founding in 1976, Habitat has built, rehabilitated, repaired or improved more than 400,000 houses worldwide, providing simple, decent and affordable shelter for more than 2 million people.

Mission

Habitat for Humanity works in partnership with God and people everywhere, from all walks of life, to develop communities with people in need by building and renovating houses so that there are decent houses in decent communities in which every person can experience Gods love and can live and grow into all that God intends.

New Beginnings

Habitat for Humanity now has a new affiliate in Pulaski County, Kentucky. After 12 months of early morning meetings and completing extensive paperwork required by Habitat for Humanity International, the local Board of Directors will be signing the final documents to become an affiliate on Thursday, July 28, 2011. The signing ceremony was performed at St. Mildreds Church and the official name of this affiliate branch will be: Habitat for Humanity of Pulaski County Kentucky.

After getting official approval from the International Habitat for Humanity office, the local board has begun the process of finding partner families, identifying lots on which to build, organizing volunteers, and fundraising. Habitat will be looking for families with a steady income at 30% of the median income and a willingness to help build their new home (sweat equity). Habitat is searching for building lots with access to city utilities (including sewer). Habitat needs a group of 30 to 50 volunteers to work on family selection, house building, and fundraising. The local board has a goal of raising $40,000 to $50,000 so they can start building the first house in March 2012.

Habitat for Humanity is a Christian based organization that works with people of all faiths, backgrounds and race. Habitat is a hand up instead of a handout to families in the lower economic classes. There previously was an affiliate in town that partnered with 5 families.
